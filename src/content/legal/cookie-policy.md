## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly, remember preferences, understand visitor behaviour, improve performance, and support marketing or advertising activities.

We may also use similar technologies such as pixels, tags, scripts, web beacons, browser storage, and tracking codes. In this Cookie Policy, we refer to all of these technologies as "cookies."

---

## 2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

- Keep our website functional, secure, and reliable.
- Remember basic preferences and settings.
- Understand how visitors use our website.
- Measure traffic, page performance, visitor interactions, and campaign performance.
- Improve our website content, layout, and user experience.
- Support advertising, remarketing, and conversion tracking where permitted.

## 3. Types of Cookies We Use

### Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are required for the website to work properly. They may support core functions such as page navigation, security, form submission, consent management, and access to secure areas of the website.

### Functional or Preference Cookies

These cookies help the website remember choices you make, such as preferences, settings, or previous interactions.

### Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website, including pages visited, time spent on pages, links clicked, traffic sources, device type, browser type, and approximate location.

### Marketing and Advertising Cookies

These cookies may be used to measure advertising performance, track conversions, build remarketing audiences, and deliver more relevant advertising across platforms.

### Social Media Cookies

These cookies may be used when our website includes social media links, embedded content, pixels, or integrations from social media platforms.

## 6. How Long Cookies Last

Cookies may be either session cookies or persistent cookies.

**Session cookies** are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser.

**Persistent cookies** remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. The duration depends on the cookie provider, purpose, and platform settings.
